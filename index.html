Dance Levels


Level One

For children aged 3-5 is typically designed to introduce young kids to basic movement and rhythm while encouraging creativity and self-expression. These classes are structured to be fun and engaging, focusing on developing motor skills, coordination, and body awareness.

Level Two

For kids aged 6-8 is all about having fun while improving their skills. The class might include games, playful exercises, and upbeat music to keep kids excited about dancing. They’ll get to try out fun moves like spins, jumps, and even little group routines. It’s about expressing themselves through dance and building confidence while learning new tricks.

Level Three

For ages 9-17 is a class where students refine their basic techniques and work on more complex choreography. Focus is on improving strength, flexibility, rhythm, and performance skills across various dance styles. You’ll perfect your technique, learn more impressive choreography, and build strength, flexibility, and confidence while having fun dancing in different styles.

Ballet is a highly technical form of dance characterized by precise movements, formalized positions, and a focus on grace, strength, and flexibility. It emphasizes balance, posture, and the execution of intricate footwork and choreography, often performed to slow or classical music.


Contemporary dance is a beautiful, expressive art form that allows dancers to tell stories through movement, breaking traditional boundaries and embracing emotion, creativity, and individuality. It's a way to connect with oneself and others, using the body as a powerful tool for expression.


Jazz dance is an energetic and expressive style that blends rhythm, movement, and emotion, often characterized by dynamic, vibrant, and fluid motions. It's a celebration of personal style and musicality.


Tap dance is a rhythmic dance form where performers create sounds by striking the floor with metal-tipped shoes. It combines intricate footwork, timing, and musicality, often used to express beats, patterns, and emotions through the sound of the taps.


Lyrical dance is a beautiful and expressive dance style that blends elements of ballet and jazz, focusing on conveying the emotional depth of a song through movement. It emphasizes fluidity, grace, and storytelling, with dancers using their bodies to interpret the lyrics and mood of the music. The style allows for personal expression, often evoking a sense of passion, or joy, as it combines technical skill with heartfelt emotion.


Hip-hop dance is a dynamic and diverse style that originated in the streets and is closely linked to hip-hop culture. It includes various techniques such as breaking, locking, popping, and more. The dance emphasizes creativity, individual style, and rhythm, and is deeply influenced by the music and the environment from which it emerged.
